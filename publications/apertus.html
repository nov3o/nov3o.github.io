Abstract

The paper introduces Apertus, the biggest European open-source language model, a comprehensive suite addressing two key gaps in the current ecosystem: data compliance and multilingual support.

The models are trained exclusively on openly available data with retroactively respecting robots.txt exclusions and filtering for non-permissive, toxic, and personally identifiable content. To reduce memorization risks, the team employs the Goldfish objective during pretraining.

The models expand language representation by training on 15 trillion tokens across over 1,800 languages, dedicating roughly 40% of pretraining data to non-English content. Available in 8B and 70B parameter sizes, Apertus achieves competitive results among fully open models on multilingual benchmarks.

Beyond model weights, the team releases all scientific artifacts from our development cycle with a permissive license, including data preparation scripts, checkpoints, evaluation suites, and training code, enabling transparent audit and extension. This approach emphasizes reproducibility, ethical data practices, and broad linguistic accessibility in open-source language model development.
